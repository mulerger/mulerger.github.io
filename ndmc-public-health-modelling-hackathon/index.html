NDMC Public Health Policy Modelling Hackathon 2026

Bridging modelling and policy for better public-health decisions

1–6 July 2026, Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Hosted by Ethiopian Public Health Institute

The NDMC Public Health Policy Modelling Hackathon brought together public-health decision-makers, programme experts, modellers, data scientists and developers to work on priority questions for Ethiopia. Over six days, four multidisciplinary families developed initial analytical and modelling prototypes with programme input.

Background

Public-health decisions increasingly depend on timely analysis that combines surveillance, programme, population and contextual data. Useful evidence emerges when decision-makers, domain specialists, modellers and developers shape questions and solutions together.

NDMC convened the hackathon to apply this collaborative approach to immunisation, malaria and measles priorities.

Hackathon approach

The programme moved from policy-question framing and family formation to collaborative analysis, model development, prototype testing and decision-maker review. The final day brought the families together to present their work, receive policy feedback and agree on follow-up.

View the complete six-day programme.

Partners and support

The hackathon was organised by the Ethiopian Public Health Institute through NDMC as part of the Strengthening Modelling Capacity to Inform Ethiopian Health Policies project, supported by the Gates Foundation. Project HOPE Ethiopia supported event implementation.
