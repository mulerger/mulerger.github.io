NDMC Public Health Policy Modelling Hackathon 2026

Bridging modelling and policy for better public-health decisions

1–6 July 2026 · Stay Easy Plus Hotel, Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

The NDMC Public Health Policy Modelling Hackathon was a six-day collaborative event bringing together public-health programme teams, modellers, data scientists, researchers and software developers to build practical, open and reproducible prototypes around priority health questions in Ethiopia.

Background

Public-health decisions increasingly require timely analysis that combines surveillance, programme, population and contextual data. Turning those data into useful policy evidence also requires close collaboration between decision-makers, domain specialists, modellers and developers.

The National Data Management and Analytics Center for Health at the Ethiopian Public Health Institute convened this hackathon to connect those skills. Four families worked on district-level zero-dose burden, antimalarial medicine forecasting and allocation, measles outbreak alert rules, and measles transmission modelling.

Who participated

The hackers represented universities, research institutes, government programmes and technology organisations across Ethiopia. Each family combined public-health knowledge with statistical analysis, mathematical modelling, data engineering and software development. Organisers and technical mentors supported challenge framing, Git/GitHub collaboration, interpretation and documentation.
