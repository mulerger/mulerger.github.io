About the hackathon

From public-health questions to working prototypes.

The hackathon was convened by the National Data Management and Analytics Center for Health at the Ethiopian Public Health Institute. Families worked with programme leads and technical mentors to clarify each challenge, review available data, develop code and prototypes, and prepare a clear policy or programme message.

The website keeps the event information, family workspaces, materials and outputs in one simple place.

Family workspaces

One branch for each challenge family.

Each family has a dedicated branch for code, documentation, slides, outputs and approved data materials. Work stays separate during development and can be submitted to the shared consolidation branch when ready.

Programme

The six-day journey.

A focused progression from challenge framing and team formation to prototype development, presentation and follow-up.

Day 1

Frame and form

Opening, challenge presentations, clarification, skills matching and family formation.

Day 2

Plan and set up

Git/GitHub refresher, dialogue with decision-makers, repository setup and work planning.

Day 3

Build

Data preparation, exploratory analysis, modelling workflow and first prototype.

Day 4

Review and refine

Prototype refinement, programme feedback, interpretation and documentation.

Day 5

Finalize

Final analysis, demonstration flow, policy message, slides and submission readiness.

Day 6

Present and continue

Family presentations, feedback, recognition, closing and agreement on next steps.
