NDMC Public Health Policy Modelling Hackathon 2026

Hackathon Overview

Bridging modelling and policy to solve public-health challenges

Mulugeta Geremew Geleso · Lead Organiser and Lead Facilitator

Purpose

Connect modelling to programme decisions

Programme need

A priority question requiring clearer evidence for planning, targeting or resource use.

Shared modelling sprint

Programme leads, facilitators and families combine questions, data and methods.

Prototype for action

A reviewable output that can be tested, improved and connected to a decision.

Objectives

What each family set out to achieve

  • Turn one priority public-health question into a clear analytical task.
  • Use available data to build a model, map, dashboard, threshold, allocation or scenario prototype.
  • Document assumptions, definitions, uncertainty and limitations.
  • Translate technical results into a practical programme message.
  • Identify the next step for refinement, adoption or institutional use.

Six-day workflow

From policy question to final demonstration

Day 1

Frame the question and form families.

Day 2

Plan the work and set up repositories.

Day 3

Prepare data and build the first prototype.

Day 4

Test with programme decision-makers.

Day 5

Refine, document and rehearse.

Day 6

Present findings and next steps.

The workflow was iterative: families tested clarity and policy relevance before the final day.

Family working model

One shared workspace, four complementary lenses

Programme

Decision context, target users, indicators and intended use.

Technical

Analysis, modelling, code, validation and visualization.

Evidence

Data inventory, quality gaps, assumptions and uncertainty.

Decision message

What the result suggests—and what it cannot yet say.

Submission package

A complete and reusable family workspace

manuscript/

Manuscript-style report covering background, methods, results, discussion, limitations and recommendations.

code/

Data preparation, estimation, mapping and validation scripts.

data/

Data dictionary, access instructions and approved public or de-identified data.

documentation/

Methods, assumptions, limitations and user guidance.

prototype/

Dashboard, application or other working decision-support prototype.

outputs/

Final tables, maps, figures and application files.

slides/

Final family presentation.

Final demonstration

Show one clear evidence pathway

  • The policy question is specific and linked to a programme action.
  • The data sources and important quality limits are visible.
  • The method is explained in plain language.
  • The prototype can be demonstrated and reviewed.
  • The result is interpreted with appropriate uncertainty.
  • The recommendation identifies a practical next step.
