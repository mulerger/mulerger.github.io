Hello there!

I’m Mulugeta Geremew Geleso, a mathematical and statistical modeller working at the intersection of public health analytics, disease modelling, geospatial intelligence, forecasting, and data-to-policy translation.

I lead the Data Analytics, Modelling and Visualization Division at the National Data Management and Analytics Center for Health, Ethiopian Public Health Institute. My work focuses on turning health data into decision-ready evidence for preparedness, programme planning, and policy action.

My current work spans infectious disease modelling, outbreak analytics, vaccine impact, geospatial risk analysis, and evidence translation for malaria, measles, TB, immunization, and broader health systems priorities.

About

Applied modelling for public health decisions

I develop and translate analytical evidence that helps public health programmes understand risk, prepare for outbreaks, target interventions, and communicate findings clearly to decision-makers.

My approach combines rigorous modelling with practical engagement: the question should be policy-relevant, the method should be transparent, and the result should be usable.

Current focus

What I work on

Disease modelling and risk analysis

Modelling infectious disease patterns, population risk, intervention scenarios, and uncertainty to support planning and preparedness.

Geospatial intelligence and forecasting

Using surveillance, population, climate, and spatial data to identify hotspots, anticipate trends, and guide targeted action.

Vaccine and immunization analytics

Generating evidence for vaccination strategy, campaign planning, vaccine impact, and policy-relevant prioritization.

Capacity building and collaboration

Supporting analysts, fellows, programmes, and partners through training, mentorship, reproducible workflows, and applied modelling collaboration.
