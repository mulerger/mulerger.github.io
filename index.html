Profile

Turning complex health data into decisions that matter.

My work sits at the intersection of epidemiology, modelling, analytics, visualization, and policy engagement.

Evidence should not stop at analysis.

It must be translated into the language of decision-makers, connected to programme priorities, and used to improve planning, preparedness, and public health impact.

Mulugeta Geremew Geleso is a mathematical and statistical modeller with experience applying epidemiological modelling, Bayesian methods, geospatial analysis, machine learning, forecasting, and visualization to strengthen public health decision-making.


He leads modelling and analytics initiatives at the National Data Management and Analytics Center for Health, Ethiopian Public Health Institute, supporting national priorities including malaria, measles, TB, immunization, outbreak preparedness, health systems analytics, and policy-oriented evidence generation.
