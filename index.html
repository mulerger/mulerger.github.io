Hello there!

I’m Mulugeta Geremew Geleso, a mathematical and statistical modeller working at the intersection of public health analytics, disease modelling, geospatial intelligence, and data-to-policy translation.

I lead the Data Analytics, Modelling and Visualization Division at the National Data Management and Analytics Center for Health, Ethiopian Public Health Institute. My work focuses on turning health data into better decisions for preparedness, programme planning, and policy action.

I work across disease modelling, outbreak analytics, forecasting, dashboards, and evidence translation, with a focus on malaria, measles, TB, immunization, and health systems analytics.

About

Public health modelling for practical decisions

My work is simple in purpose: help programmes and decision-makers use data more effectively. I combine modelling, analytics, visualization, and policy engagement to produce outputs that are technically strong and practically useful.

  • Disease modelling: transmission, burden, intervention, and scenario analysis.
  • Geospatial analytics: risk mapping, spatial targeting, and surveillance intelligence.
  • Forecasting: early warning, outbreak preparedness, and programme planning.
  • Evidence translation: briefs, dashboards, presentations, and decision-support products.
